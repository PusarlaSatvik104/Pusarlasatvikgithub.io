              <h2 class="s-title">VLSI Design</h2>
              <p class="s-description text-center">
               Very-Large-Scale Integration (VLSI) refers to the process of creating integrated circuits (ICs) by combining thousands to millions of transistors into a single chip. It is a critical technology in modern electronics, enabling the development of complex devices such as microprocessors, memory chips, and specialized digital systems. VLSI allows for higher performance, lower power consumption, and smaller form factors in electronic devices, which are essential for applications ranging from smartphones to advanced computing systems. It plays a foundational role in fields like computer engineering, telecommunications, and embedded systems

              <h2 class="s-title">Analog Design</h2>
              <p class="s-description text-center">
               Analog refers to a type of signal or system that represents data as continuous, variable quantities, rather than discrete values like in digital systems. In analog systems, the signal varies smoothly over time, which allows for the accurate representation of physical phenomena such as sound, light, temperature, and voltage. Analog circuits, commonly found in devices like radios, amplifiers, and sensors, process these signals using components like resistors, capacitors, and transistors. Though digital technology is more prevalent today, analog remains essential for interfacing with the real world, where many signals naturally occur in continuous form.               </p>
